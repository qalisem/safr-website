Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 28, 2026

Overview

Safr ("we," "our," or "the app") is a navigation app designed for Muslim travelers. We are committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and your rights.

Data We Collect

Location data — Safr uses your device's GPS to provide navigation, calculate prayer times for your current position, determine Qibla direction, and find nearby mosques. Location data is processed on your device and sent to mapping services (Mapbox, Google Places) only as needed for routing and place search. We do not store your location history on our servers.

Locally stored preferences — Your settings (prayer calculation method, madhab, corridor width, saved locations like Home/Work, recent destinations) are stored locally on your device using SharedPreferences. This data never leaves your device.

No accounts or personal information — Safr does not require an account. We do not collect your name, email, phone number, or any other personal identifiers.

Third-Party Services

Safr uses the following third-party services to function:

  • Mapbox — Map display, turn-by-turn navigation, and geocoding. Subject to Mapbox's Privacy Policy.
  • Google Places API — Place search and business information. Subject to Google's Privacy Policy.
  • OpenStreetMap — Mosque location data sourced from the OSM community database. Subject to the OSM Foundation Privacy Policy. Data © OpenStreetMap contributors.

These services receive location coordinates as needed to provide their functionality. We do not share any additional data with them.

Data We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not track your browsing or app usage behavior
  • We do not sell or share data with advertisers
  • We do not use analytics or tracking SDKs
  • We do not store your routes, destinations, or travel history on any server

Children's Privacy

Safr is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. The "last updated" date at the top will reflect any changes. Continued use of Safr after updates constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

Terms of Use

Last updated: February 28, 2026

Acceptance

By downloading or using Safr, you agree to these terms. If you do not agree, do not use the app.

What Safr Is

Safr is a navigation app that integrates Islamic prayer times and mosque locations into route planning. It is designed to help Muslim travelers plan prayer stops during road trips.

What Safr Is NOT

Safr is not a replacement for your own judgment while driving. Prayer times are calculated algorithmically and may differ slightly from your local mosque's announced times. Mosque information is sourced from public databases and may not always be current (hours, open/closed status, etc.).

Safe Driving

Do not interact with the app while driving. Set up your route before you start driving. Voice guidance and prayer alerts are designed to minimize the need to look at your screen. You are solely responsible for safe operation of your vehicle.

Accuracy

We strive for accuracy in prayer times, mosque locations, and routing, but we cannot guarantee perfection. Prayer times are calculated using established North American astronomical methods (ISNA, MWL) and may vary by 1-2 minutes from other sources. Mosque data comes from OpenStreetMap and Google Places and may contain inaccuracies.

Availability

Safr is currently available in the United States and Canada. Mosque coverage and routing quality may vary by region. The app requires an internet connection for navigation and mosque search.
